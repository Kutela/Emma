Emmanuel Kidando Ph.D.


About me


I am currently a PhD candidate in civil and transportation engineering at Florida State University working with Dr. Ren Moses.

I am a passionate civil engineer and data scientist who has broad in-depth knowledge in transportation engineering, programming, and statistical skills. I am using these skills to solve traffic engineering problems by exploring machine learning, Bayesian modeling, data visualization, etc. I have more than 4 years of experience in data analytics, data mining and developing predictive models using tools such as Python, R, Julia, etc.

My current research focuses on connected vehicle (CV) technology with emphasis in deployment, testing, data analytics and visualization of CVs field data. I have also performed research in the area of traffic operations, Automated Traffic Signal Performance Measures (ATSPMs), safety, and simulation.
